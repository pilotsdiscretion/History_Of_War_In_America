# History of War in America, 1775–2026

An interactive, single-page data visualization of every major U.S. war and armed conflict from the Revolution through the post-9/11 wars to the proxy and shadow conflicts in Ukraine and with Iran. Each conflict has its own tab covering the years of the war, a background passage on how and why it began and who the main actors were, both-sides casualties and deaths, munitions and forces, war crimes and atrocities, costs, the congressional authorization (or lack of one) with voting records, conscription, and the contractors who profited. Cross-cutting tabs cover conscription and the draft, war powers and authorizations, and the rise of drone warfare. Every figure is sourced to a companion dossier.

## What's inside

The dashboard is organized into topic tabs, in chronological order with **Conclusion** last:

Overview · Revolutionary War · Northwest Indian War · Quasi-War · First/Second Barbary Wars · War of 1812 · Seminole Wars · Black Hawk War · Mexican–American War · American Indian / Plains Wars · Civil War · Spanish–American War · Philippine–American War · Boxer Rebellion · Banana Wars · Mexican Border War · World War I · World War II · Korea · Lebanon (1958) · Bay of Pigs · Vietnam · Dominican Intervention · Lebanon (1982–84) · Grenada · Libya (1986) · Panama · Gulf War · Somalia · Bosnia · Haiti · Kosovo · Afghanistan · Iraq · Libya (2011) · War Against ISIS · U.S.–Iran Conflict (1979–) · Ukraine (U.S. proxy war, 2022–) · **Conscription & the Draft** · **War Powers & Authorizations** · **Drones & UAVs** · **Private Military Contractors** · **Conclusion**

Each conflict tab combines headline statistics, a **Background & Why the U.S. Fought** passage (start, causes, and the primary actors with names, titles and country), a **Cost to U.S. taxpayers** callout (inflation-adjusted and nominal, with the basis noted), a **both-sides breakdown** (deaths, wounded, forces, cost), charts, a **Major Battles** history, a war-crimes/atrocities callout where applicable, an authorization badge with the relevant act and congressional vote, conscription notes, and a **War Contractors & Profiteers** section (including stock and market-cap data for publicly-traded primes from WWI onward).

### Highlighted coverage

- **College-course depth on every tab** — each conflict reads as a complete brief: who fought and why (a **Background** and, for the major wars, a **Course of the War**), how it ended, and an **Outcome & Lasting Impact** synthesis of how it changed America going forward; the cross-cutting tabs close with a **Legacy & Why It Matters Today**.
- **Both sides analyzed** — U.S. and adversary casualties, deaths, wounded, forces, and costs side by side, with enemy and civilian tolls shown as ranges where they are contested.
- **Cost to taxpayers** — every conflict tab states the war's cost to U.S. taxpayers (inflation-adjusted constant dollars where available, plus the nominal figure and basis), from the Revolution's ~$2.4B to WWII's ~$4.1T and the ~$8T post-9/11 wars, and **charts that cost against other U.S. wars** on a log scale.
- **Congressional war powers** — the five formal declarations of war and every major authorization (Gulf of Tonkin, 1991 Gulf, 2001 & 2002 AUMFs) with House and Senate vote tallies, plus the 1973 War Powers Resolution.
- **Conscription** — the draft from the Civil War through the World Wars, Korea, and Vietnam's lottery, to the 1973 all-volunteer force, with induction figures.
- **War contractors & profiteers** — from the Revolution's privateers and the Civil War's "shoddy," through the WWI "merchants of death" and WWII "Arsenal of Democracy," to Halliburton/KBR in Iraq — with revenue, profit, market cap, and stock charts for the modern primes.
- **Drones & UAVs** — the unmanned turn in American warfare, from Vietnam-era reconnaissance drones to armed Predator/Reaper strikes by country and administration.
- **Private military contractors** — a dedicated tab on the privatization of war: Blackwater (Erik Prince), Triple Canopy, DynCorp and Constellis, their use **abroad** (Iraq/Afghanistan, where contractors outnumbered troops and the Nisour Square massacre and CPA Order 17 immunity exposed an accountability gap) and **at home** (Blackwater in New Orleans after Hurricane Katrina; the Moyock, NC training campus), with contractor death tolls, the State Department's WPS security contracts, and the scandals from Nisour Square to DynCorp's Bosnia case. It tells the full arc of the **"Blackwater Four"** (the Nisour Square guards' action, 2014 conviction, appeals, and 2020 presidential pardon) and gives an evenhanded account of the **2012 Benghazi attack** — the contractors who guarded the mission and CIA Annex, the denied security requests and Accountability Review Board findings, the disputed "stand-down," and the political fallout.
- **Proxy & shadow wars** — Ukraine (the U.S. arming and funding Kyiv against Russia with no U.S. combat troops — ~$175B committed) and the U.S.–Iran confrontation (the 1979 hostage crisis, the 1988 Tanker War, the 2020 Soleimani strike, and the 2025 nuclear-site strikes), with the most recent events flagged as contested and still-evolving.
- **Major battles & a deep WWII dive** — every conflict tab has a Major Battles history; World War II, the largest U.S. war, gets the fullest treatment — both the European and Pacific theaters and a chart of U.S. deaths in its costliest battles (the Bulge, Normandy, Okinawa, Guadalcanal, Iwo Jima).
- **Notable events & in the media** — each conflict tab highlights the incidents and operations that made international headlines (Black Hawk Down, Lone Survivor, My Lai, Abu Ghraib, the bin Laden raid, and more) and the films and images they're remembered by.
- **War crimes & atrocities** — alleged and prosecuted war crimes for each conflict, including the special-forces ("Tier 1") murder cases in Iraq and Afghanistan across the U.S. (Bales, Gallagher, the 2019 pardons), Australia (the Brereton Report; Ben Roberts-Smith) and the U.K. (the SAS inquiry).

## Features

- **Mobile-responsive** — the layout adapts to phones and tablets; the topic menu collapses on small screens.
- **Collapsible topic header** — a "☰ Conflicts" toggle shows or hides the full tab list.
- **Click-to-zoom charts** — every chart has a zoom (⤢) control that opens it full-screen.
- **Sourced throughout** — every chart, stat, and "Key Timeline Events" entry carries a clickable "source ↗" link to its section on the Sources & Citations page.

## Data & methodology

Figures come from the responsible authority for each domain — the VA's "America's Wars," the DoD Defense Casualty Analysis System, the Congressional Research Service's "Costs of Major U.S. Wars," congressional roll-call records, the Watson Institute's "Costs of War," SEC filings and market data for contractors, and academic histories. War casualty, cost, and munitions figures are frequently **estimates that are contested and revised across sources** — especially enemy and civilian deaths, early-era figures, and irregular conflicts. Where figures diverge, the relevant chart's data note says so. Always verify any figure against the cited primary source before relying on it.

## Tech

Plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with [Chart.js](https://www.chartjs.org/) for visualizations. No framework, no build tooling, no tracking.
