Source Dossier · Primary-Source Documentation

Sources & Citations

Authoritative references backing every figure in “History of War in America From 1775–2025.”

Compiled: June 16, 2026 Coverage: 40+ conflicts, conscription, war powers & congressional votes, drones, and war contractors Basis: DoD/DMDC, VA, CRS, OMB/CBO, Congress.gov roll-calls, SEC/EDGAR & academic histories

Purpose. This dossier documents the primary source behind each figure in the dashboard, so that any number can be traced to a responsible authority. Every entry lists the organization (and why it is authoritative), the exact dataset, report, or filing, the author(s), the date, a working link, and which numbers it supports.

On war figures. Casualty, cost, and munitions totals are frequently estimates that differ across sources, especially for enemy and civilian dead, irregular conflicts, and the 18th–19th centuries; where they diverge we give a range and a data note. Contractor financials are drawn from SEC filings, annual reports, and audited government data; stock prices are split-adjusted. Source type is color-tagged: GOVERNMENT U.S. federal / military statistics & records PEER-REVIEWED academic history / journal RESEARCH ORG nonpartisan institute / NGO INDUSTRY corporate / financial-market data

§Primary authorities at a glance

Most figures rest on a U.S. government record of war casualties, costs, and legislation, or on audited corporate financial filings. These are the standard references in scholarship, congressional testimony, and reporting; U.S. government data is in the public domain.
